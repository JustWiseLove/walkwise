Hebrews 10:24,25
And let us consider one another so as to incite to love and fine works, not forsaking our meeting together, as some have the custom, but encouraging one another, and all the more so as you see the day drawing near.
1. What is the purpose of Jehovah’s Witnesses’ organization?

The organization exists to help people worship Jehovah God in unity, spread the good news of His Kingdom, and support one another in living by Bible principles.

Matthew 24:14 - “And this good news of the Kingdom will be preached in all the inhabited earth for a witness to all the nations, and then the end will come.”

Hebrews 10:24-25 - “And let us consider one another so as to incite to love and fine works, not forsaking our meeting together.”

2. Who leads Jehovah’s Witnesses?

Jehovah’s Witnesses are led by Jesus Christ as their Head, with a Governing Body of anointed elders providing spiritual direction based on the Bible.

Ephesians 5:23 - “Christ is head of the congregation, he being a savior of this body.”

Acts 15:2 - “So after a dispute... they arranged for Paul and Barnabas and some others to go up to the apostles and elders in Jerusalem.”

3. How is the organization structured?

The organization includes a Governing Body overseeing global activities, branch offices managing regions, and local congregations led by elders—all working together without a paid clergy.

1 Corinthians 14:40 - “But let all things take place decently and by arrangement.”

1 Peter 5:2-3 - “Shepherd the flock of God under your care... not lording it over those who are God’s inheritance.”

4. Why don’t Jehovah’s Witnesses have a paid clergy?

The Bible shows all Christians as equal ministers, so there’s no paid clergy; elders serve voluntarily, following Jesus’ example of humility and service.

Matthew 23:8 - “But you, do not you be called Rabbi, for one is your Teacher, and all of you are brothers.”

1 Corinthians 9:18 - “That when I declare the good news, I may offer the good news without cost.”

5. How does the organization support preaching?

It provides training, literature, and tools like jw.org to equip all members to preach, ensuring the Kingdom message reaches people worldwide as Jesus commanded.

Matthew 28:19-20 - “Go, therefore, and make disciples of people of all the nations... teaching them to observe all the things I have commanded you.”

2 Timothy 4:2 - “Preach the word; be at it urgently in favorable times and in troublesome times.”

6. What role do meetings play in the organization?

Meetings strengthen faith through Bible study, encourage love and good works, and prepare members to share the truth with others.

Hebrews 10:24-25 - “Let us consider one another so as to incite to love and fine works, not forsaking our meeting together.”

Psalm 22:22 - “I will declare your name to my brothers; in the midst of the congregation I will praise you.”

7. How is the organization financed?

It relies on voluntary donations from members and supporters, not tithes or collections, following the Bible’s pattern of willing gifts for God’s work.

2 Corinthians 9:7 - “Let each one do just as he has resolved in his heart, not grudgingly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver.”

Acts 4:34-35 - “There was not a needy person among them... and distribution was made to each one according to his need.”

8. Why is unity important to the organization?

Unity reflects true worship and fulfills Jesus’ prayer for his followers to be one, making the organization a global brotherhood rooted in love and truth.

John 17:20-21 - “I make request... that they may all be one, just as you, Father, are in union with me.”

1 Corinthians 1:10 - “Now I urge you, brothers... that you should all speak in agreement and that there should be no divisions among you.”

9. How does the organization help during disasters?

It organizes practical aid like food, shelter, and emotional support for victims, reflecting God’s love and preparing members to care for others in need.

Galatians 6:10 - “So, then, as long as we have the opportunity, let us work what is good toward all, but especially toward those related to us in the faith.”

James 2:16-17 - “If a brother or a sister is lacking clothing and enough food... faith by itself, without works, is dead.”

10. How can I learn more about the organization?

You can learn more by attending our meetings or studying the Bible with us! We’re eager to show you how the organization follows God’s Word and supports people worldwide.

John 13:35 - “By this all will know that you are my disciples—if you have love among yourselves.”

Acts 17:11 - “They accepted the word with the greatest eagerness of mind, carefully examining the Scriptures daily.”
