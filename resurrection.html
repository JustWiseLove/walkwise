Resurrection

Explore Truth False Worship
John 5:28,29
Do not be amazed at this, for the hour is coming in which all those in the memorial tombs will hear his voice and come out, those who did good things to a resurrection of life, and those who practiced vile things to a resurrection of judgment.
1. What is the resurrection?

The resurrection is God’s promise to bring the dead back to life, restoring them with their unique identity, personality, and memories. It’s a real hope for billions who have died, offering them a chance to live again.

John 5:28-29 - “Do not be amazed at this, for the hour is coming in which all those in the memorial tombs will hear his voice and come out.”

Acts 24:15 - “There is going to be a resurrection of both the righteous and the unrighteous.”

2. Who will be resurrected?

Both the righteous—those who obeyed God—and the unrighteous—those who didn’t know Him—will be resurrected. However, those judged as irreformably wicked will not return to life.

Acts 24:15 - “I have hope toward God... that there is going to be a resurrection of both the righteous and the unrighteous.”

Matthew 23:33 - “Serpents, offspring of vipers, how will you flee from the judgment of Gehenna?”

3. Where will the resurrected ones live?

Most will be resurrected to life on a paradise earth, while a limited group of 144,000 will be resurrected to heaven to rule with Christ as kings and priests.

Psalm 37:29 - “The righteous will possess the earth, and they will live forever on it.”

Revelation 14:1, 3 - “The Lamb standing on Mount Zion, and with him 144,000... who have been bought from the earth.”

4. When will the resurrection happen?

The resurrection to heaven began during Christ’s presence, which started in 1914. The resurrection to earth will occur during Christ’s Thousand-Year Reign, transforming the earth into a paradise.

1 Corinthians 15:23 - “Each one in his own proper order: Christ the firstfruits, afterward those who belong to the Christ during his presence.”

Revelation 20:6 - “They will be priests of God and of the Christ, and they will rule as kings with him for the thousand years.”

5. How does God perform the resurrection?

Jehovah grants Jesus the power to raise the dead, using His perfect memory to recreate each person as they were, either with a spirit body for heaven or a physical body for earth.

John 11:25 - “Jesus said to her: ‘I am the resurrection and the life.’”

1 Corinthians 15:44 - “It is sown a physical body; it is raised up a spiritual body.”

6. Why is the resurrection reasonable?

The resurrection is reasonable because God has already demonstrated His power through nine recorded resurrections in the Bible, each witnessed by many, and He yearns to bring the dead back.

John 11:43-44 - “He cried out with a loud voice: ‘Lazarus, come out!’ The man who had been dead came out.”

Job 14:15 - “You will call, and I will answer you. You will long for the work of your hands.”

7. What happens to the soul at death?

The Bible teaches that the soul is the person, not a separate entity that survives death. At death, a person ceases to exist until resurrected, not reuniting with a soul but being recreated.

Ezekiel 18:4 - “The soul who sins is the one who will die.”

Ecclesiastes 9:5 - “The dead know nothing at all.”

8. Will the resurrected face judgment?

Yes, those resurrected to earth will face a judgment period during the Thousand-Year Reign, where their actions will determine if they gain everlasting life or face destruction.

John 5:29 - “Those who did good things to a resurrection of life, and those who practiced vile things to a resurrection of judgment.”

Revelation 20:12 - “The dead were judged out of those things written in the scrolls according to their deeds.”

9. How does Jesus’ resurrection give us hope?

Jesus’ resurrection proves God’s power over death and guarantees that others will follow. As the “firstfruits,” it confirms the promise of life for all who exercise faith in him.

1 Corinthians 15:20 - “Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep in death.”

Acts 17:31 - “He has set a day when he will judge the inhabited earth in righteousness by a man whom he has appointed.”

10. How can I learn more about the resurrection?

You can deepen your understanding by studying the Bible with us! It offers detailed accounts and promises about the resurrection, giving hope for you and your loved ones.

John 17:3 - “This means everlasting life, their coming to know you, the only true God, and the one whom you sent, Jesus Christ.”

Romans 15:4 - “All the things that were written beforehand were written for our instruction, that through our endurance and through the comfort from the Scriptures we might have hope.”
